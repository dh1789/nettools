## Why You Need a Cron Expression Tool

Cron expressions are essential for server operations, CI/CD pipelines, and batch job scheduling. But expressions like `0 */6 * * 1-5` aren't exactly easy to read at a glance.

crontab.guru has been the de facto standard for years — it's well made, and I still use it. I built the [Cron Expression Parser](/tools/net/cron-parser/) for just two reasons: explaining the English interpretation every time I shared an expression with a Korean-speaking team got tedious, and I once dropped an AWS EventBridge 6-field (with-seconds) cron into a 5-field tool, had every field shift by one, and watched a batch job fire in the middle of the night. Below is an honest "which one, when" comparison — not marketing.

## Detailed Comparison

### Korean Interpretation

beomanro interprets cron expressions **naturally in Korean**. For example, `0 9 * * 1-5` displays as "Runs at 9:00 AM on weekdays." crontab.guru supports English only.

### 6-Field Cron Support

beomanro natively supports 6-field cron expressions (including seconds), used by AWS EventBridge, Spring Scheduler, and others. crontab.guru only supports traditional 5-field cron.

### Where processing happens

Both tools interpret expressions in the browser (client-side), so your input is never sent to a server. You can paste internal scheduling expressions into either one without them leaving your machine — this is the same on both sides.

## Usage Scenarios

### When beomanro is better

- Sharing and validating cron expressions in Korean-speaking teams
- Working with 6-field cron in AWS/Spring environments
- Quick lookups with a clean, modern UI

### When crontab.guru is better

- Using only traditional 5-field Unix cron
- Comfortable with English and an established workflow

## Conclusion

If you only use 5-field cron and prefer English, crontab.guru remains an excellent choice. If you need Korean interpretation or work with AWS/Spring 6-field (with-seconds) cron, beomanro's [Cron Expression Parser](/tools/net/cron-parser/) fills exactly those two gaps.
